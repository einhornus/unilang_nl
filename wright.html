wright (17416)


Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ɹaɪt/
  • Rhymes: -aɪt
  • Homophones: right, rite, write

Etymology 1

From Middle English wrighte, wriȝte, wruhte, wurhte, from Old English ang">wyrhta (“worker; wright; workman; artificer; laborer; craftsman”), from Proto-Germanic *wurhtijô (as in *wurkijaną), from Proto-Indo-European *werǵ- (“to work”) (English work). Cognate with wrought, dated Dutch wrecht.

Noun

wright (plural wrights)

  1. (obsolete) A builder or maker of something.
