Etymology[edit]

From bevel (“command”) +‎ hebben (“to have”) +‎ -er. First known attestations from ca. 1535 and thus perhaps after German Befehl(s)haber (1490), probably already Middle High German bevelhaber (1335, copy 16th c.).
