hamlet (8397)


Etymology

From Middle English hamlet, hamelet, a borrowing from Old French hamelet, diminutive of Old French hamel (Modern French hameau), in turn diminutive of Old French ham, of Germanic origin. Cognates include English home, Dutch heem, German Heim, Old English ang">hām.

Noun

hamlet (plural hamlets)

  2. (Great Britain">Britain) A village that does not have its own church.
