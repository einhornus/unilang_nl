wieberen (48053)


Etymology

From wieberig (“zich ~ maken / ~ gaan: (Bargoens) to scarper”) +‎ -en < Yiddish ועייִבערעח‎ (veyiverekh) < Hebrew וַיִּבְרַח‎ (vayivrách, “(he) fled”)

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ʋibərə(n)/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Rhymes: -ibərən

Verb

wieberen

  1. (colloquial) to scarper, to flee
  2. (colloquial) to leave
  3. (colloquial) to go, to move
  4. (colloquial, transitive) to move, to remove
  5. (colloquial, transitive) to lay off

Usage notes

Often used in the imperative infinitive, often with a negative charge, as in: ‘Sod off!’ Also used to indicate wavering, uncertain, &c. motion. Nowadays also used transitively, in which case the active perfect is formed using the auxiliary verb hebben instead of zijn.
