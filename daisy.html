daisy (4952)


Etymology

From Middle English dayesye, from Old English ang">dæġes ēage (“daisy”, literally “day's eye”) due to the flowers closing their blossoms during night. The rhyming slang comes from daisy roots for boots.

Pronunciation

  • (British English">UK) IPA(key): /ˈdeɪ.zi/
  • Audio (US)
    		(file)
  • Rhymes: -eɪzi

Noun

daisy (plural daisies)

  1. A wild flowering plant Bellis perennis of the Asteraceae family, with a yellow head and white petals
  2. Many other flowering plants of various species.
  3. (Cockney rhyming slang) A boot or other footwear.
