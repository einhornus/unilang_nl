contention (45690)

<br>

Etymology[edit]

From Middle English contencion, borrowed from Old French contencion, from Latin contentio, contentionem, from contendō (past participle contentus); see contend.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /kənˈtɛnʃən/
  • Hyphenation: con‧ten‧tion

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">contention (countable and uncountable, plural contentions)

<ol>
  • Argument, contest, debate, strife, struggle.
  • A point maintained in an argument, or a line of argument taken in its support; the subject matter of discussion of strife; a position taken or contended for.
    It is my contention that state lotteries are taxes on stupid people.
  • (computing, telecommunications) Competition by parts of a system or its users for a limited resource.

    • Synonyms[edit]

    • gainstrife, gainstriving, wrangling
