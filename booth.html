booth (5670)


Etymology

From English">Middle English bothe, from Old Norse">Old Norse non">bóð (compare Swedish bod) and/or the commoner variant non">búð (> Scots buth), from Proto-Germanic *bōþō, *būþiz, *buþǭ, from Proto-Indo-European *BʰuH-. Compare Low German">Middle Low German bôde, Middle Dutch boede, German Bude.

Pronunciation

  • (England) IPA(key): /buːð/
  • (General American">General American) IPA(key): /buːθ/
  • Rhymes: -uːθ, -uːð

Noun

booth (plural booths)

  1. A small stall for the display and sale of goods.
  2. An enclosure just big enough to accommodate one standing person.
  3. An enclosed table with seats, as in a diner or café.
  4. An enclosure for keeping animals.
