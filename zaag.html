Pronunciation

  • audio

  • Hyphenation: zaag
  • Rhymes: -aːx

Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch sage, from Old Dutch *saga, from Proto-Germanic *sagō, from Proto-Indo-European *sek- (“to cut”).

Noun

zaag f (plural zagen, diminutive zaagje n)

  1. saw (cutting tool)
  2. nagger, whiner (annoying person)

Etymology 2

See the etymology of the main entry.

Verb

zaag

  1. first-person singular present indicative of zagen
  2. imperative of zagen
