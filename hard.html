  • hard

  • Hyphenation: hard
  • Rhymes: -ɑrt
  • Homophone: hart

Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch hart, from Old Dutch hart, from Proto-Germanic *harduz.

Adjective

hard (comparative harder, superlative hardst)

  1. hard, strong
    Antonym: zacht
  2. (economics, of a currency) strong, not easily devalued
  3. unquestionable, uncontestable
    harde feiten
    hard facts
  4. heartless, unsympathetic (of a person)
    Antonym: zacht
  5. hard, difficult
    een harde strijd
    a difficult fight
  6. harsh, heavy
    harde straffen
    harsh punishments
    een harde regen
    heavy rain
  7. hard, rich in calcium (of water)
    Antonym: zacht
  8. loud (of sound)
    Synonym: luid
    Antonym: zacht
