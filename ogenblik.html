Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch ogenblic, from Middle Low German ōgenblik. Compare Norwegian øyeblikk, Danish øjeblik, German Augenblick, Icelandic augnablik, Swedish ögonblick, Yiddish אויגנבליק‎ (oygnblik), and English eyeblink. Equivalent to oog (“eye”) +‎ -en- +‎ blik (“look”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˌoː.ɣənˈblɪk/
  • ogenblik
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: ogen‧blik

Noun[edit]

ogenblik n (plural ogenblikken, diminutive ogenblikje n)

  1. moment, blink of an eye (instant of time)
