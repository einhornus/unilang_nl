aubergine (30077)



Etymology

Borrowed from French aubergine, from Catalan albergínia, from Arabic اَلْبَاذِنْجَان (al-bāḏinjān, “the eggplant”), from Persian بادنجان (bâdenjân), from باتنجان (bâtenjân), from Sanskrit वातिगगम (vātiga-gama, “eggplant”, literally “the plant that cures the wind”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˌoːbɛrˈʒinə/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: au‧ber‧gi‧ne
  • Rhymes: -inə

Noun

aubergine f (plural aubergines, diminutive auberginetje n)

  1. An aubergine, an eggplant (plant), Solanum melongena.
  2. The fruit of the eggplant, an aubergine (fruit).
  3. Aubergine, eggplant (color).

Zelfstandig naamwoord

aubergine v

  1. (plantkunde) Solanum melongena , plant uit de nachtschadefamilie
  2. (groente) purperachtige, vlezige vrucht van deze plant
    • Koop voor mij even twee aubergines in de winkel.
