Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch laet, from Old Dutch *lat, from Proto-Germanic *lataz.

Adjective

laat (comparative later, superlative laatst)

  1. late (not early)
    Het is toch te laat.
    It's too late.
    't Is al laat geworden.
    It's gotten late already.
  2. tardy
    Ze komt altijd laat aan.
    She always arrives late.
