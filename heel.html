Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch hêel, from Old Dutch hēl, from Proto-Germanic *hailaz.

Adjective

heel (comparative heler, superlative heelst)

  1. complete, full, whole
  2. unbroken, undamaged, untarnished
  3. big, enormous, significant

Adverb

heel

  1. very
Usage notes

Although an adverb, heel may be inflected as well (hele) to match the following adjective, by analogy with the inflection of adjectives in Dutch.

. For example, both of these sentences are correct:

  • Dat is een heel grote boom.
    That is a very large tree.
  • Dat is een hele grote boom.
    That is a very large tree.

The latter form may be regarded as informal and less appropriate for formal writing.
