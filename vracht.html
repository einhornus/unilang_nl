vracht (7636)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch vracht, from Middle Low German vracht, vrecht (“payment for cargo”), from Saxon">Old Saxon frāht, frēht. Displaced the native Middle Dutch vrecht, both originally deriving from Proto-Germanic *fra- + *aihtiz. Cognate with English freight and fraught.

Pronunciation

  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Rhymes: -ɑxt

Noun

vracht f (plural vrachten, diminutive vrachtje n)

  1. freight
  2. a large amount, a mass

Zelfstandig naamwoord

vracht m

  1. de lading van een vervoermiddel
Verwante begrippen
  • cargo, goederen, lading, last, scheepslading, vervoer
