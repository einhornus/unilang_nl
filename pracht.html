pracht (10652)


Etymology

From Middle High German praht, southern variety of braht, from Old High German braht, praht, from Proto-Germanic *brahtaz (“cracking, snapping, noise”). Related to breken (“to break, to snap”).

Pronunciation

  • Rhymes: -ɑxt

Noun

pracht f (uncountable)
