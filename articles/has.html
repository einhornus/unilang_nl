has (82899)


Etymology

From Dutch haas (“tenderloin”), from Middle Dutch haessen, from Old Dutch *hāsenewa, *hāhsenewa, from Proto-Germanic *hanhsenwō, *hanhasenwō (“heel tendon, Achilles tendon”), from Proto-Germanic *hanhaz (“heel”) + *senwō (“sinew”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): [ˈhas]
  • Hyphenation: has

Noun

has

  1. tenderloin.
