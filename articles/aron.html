aron (16156)


Etymology 1

Borrowed from Latin aron (also arum), from Ancient Greek ἄρον (áron).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈaː.rɔn/
  • Hyphenation: aron

Noun

aron m (plural arons)

  1. (obsolete) arum, plant or flower of the genus Arum.
Derived terms
  • aronskelk

Etymology 2

Borrowed from Hebrew אָרוֹן.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /aːˈroːn/
  • Hyphenation: aron

Noun

aron m (plural arons or aroniem)

  1. (Judaism) ark (consecrated receptable for storing Torah scrolls)
    Synonym: Aron Hakodesj

Zelfstandig naamwoord

aron v/m

  1. (Jiddisch-Hebreeuws) ark in de synagoge (kast voor de Torarollen)
  2. (Jiddisch-Hebreeuws) doodskist
