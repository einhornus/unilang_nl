in. (13291)


Etymology

From Old Dutch in, from Proto-Germanic *in, from Proto-Indo-European *en.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ɪn/
  • audio
    		(file)
  • Rhymes: -ɪn

Adverb

in

  1. in, inside
  2. (postpositional) into
    De jongen rende het huis in.
    The boy ran into the house.
