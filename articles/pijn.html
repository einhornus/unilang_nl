Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch pine, from Latin poena (“punishment, strife”), from Ancient Greek ποινή (poinḗ, “bloodmoney, weregild, fine, price paid, penalty”).

Noun

pijn f (plural pijnen, diminutive pijntje n)

  1. physical pain, ache
  2. psychological pain, hurt

Etymology 2

From Middle Dutch pijn, from Latin pīnus.

Noun

pijn m (plural pijnen, diminutive pijntje n)

  1. (rare) pine (tree)
Synonyms

  • denneboom

Derived terms
