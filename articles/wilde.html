wilde (173)


Pronunciation

  • Audio
    		(file)

Etymology 1

From wild.

Adjective

wilde

  1. Inflected form of wild

Noun

wilde m or f (plural wilden)

  1. savage, uncivilized person
  2. brute
Synonyms
  • (savage): wildeman
  • (brute): bruut, woesteling
Derived terms
  • halve wilde
  • wildenhorde
  • wildenras

Etymology 2

Verb

wilde

  1. singular past indicative and subjunctive of willen
Synonyms
  • wou

Etymology 3

Noun

wilde f (uncountable)

  1. Alternative form of weelde

Etymology 4

From Middle Dutch wildi, a contraction of wilt gi (modern wilt gij).

Contraction

wilde

  1. (Brabant) Contraction of wilt gij.
Usage notes

The contraction is sometimes reinforced with an additional gij, giving wilde gij.
