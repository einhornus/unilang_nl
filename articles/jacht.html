Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch jacht, jaget, from Old Dutch *jagot, from Proto-West Germanic *jagōþu (“hunt, hunting”).

Noun

jacht f (uncountable)

  1. hunt, hunting

Etymology 2

Shortening of jachtschip.

Noun

jacht n (plural jachten, diminutive jachtje n)

  1. yacht (fast, light and now often luxurious (sailing) boat) [from early 16th c.]
Derived terms
