dwingeland (49643)


Etymology

From dwingen (“to coerce”) + -e- + land (“country”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈdʋɪ.ŋəˌlɑnt/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: dwin‧ge‧land

Noun

dwingeland m (plural dwingelanden, diminutive dwingelandje n)

  1. tyrant, despotic ruler who enforces his will upon the state
    Synonyms: despoot, dictator, tiran
  2. (figurative) dictatorial person, who is bossy and coerces others to obedient compliance

Derived terms

  • dwingelandij
