  • Rhymes: -eːkən

Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch stēken, from Old Dutch *stecan, from Proto-Germanic *stikaną, originally a class 1 strong verb.

Verb

steken

  1. (transitive) to prick, sting
  2. (transitive) to stab, thrust (with a sharp instrument such as a dagger)
  3. (transitive) to insert, put in
    Ik hoorde hoe een sleutel in het slot werd gestoken.
    I heard a key being inserted into the keyhole.
