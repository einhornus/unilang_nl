ploertendoder (101222)


Etymology

Compound of ploert (“twit, bounder”) + -en- (interfix) + doder (“killer”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈpluːr.tə(n)ˌdoː.dər/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: ploer‧ten‧do‧der

Noun

ploertendoder m (plural ploertendoders)

  1. A cosh, blackjack or similar flexible blunt weapon containing heavy material, often but not necessarily covered with rubber or leather.
