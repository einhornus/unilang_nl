zwabbergast (96455)


Etymology

From zwabber (“a mop”) + gast (“guest; apprentice; one who mans a piece of equipment”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈzʋɑ.bərˌɣɑst/
  • Hyphenation: zwab‧ber‧gast

Noun

zwabbergast m (plural zwabbergasten, diminutive zwabbergastje n)

  1. A cabin-boy; a junior sailor, especially an apprentice, who does most of the chores, such as mopping.

Synonyms

  • zwabberkapitein
  • zwabberpaai

Related terms

  • zwabberbak
  • zwabberen
  • gezwabber
