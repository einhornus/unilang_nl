Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch meren, from Old Dutch *mēron, from Proto-Germanic *mairōną, related to *maida- (“post”), from Proto-Indo-European *mēyt-, *meyt-, from *mēy-, *mey- (“stake, pole”). Cognate with English moor.

Verb[edit]

meren

  1. (transitive or intransitive) to moor
