Etymology 1

English Wikipedia has an article on:
brill (fish)
Wikipedia

Possibly from Cornish brilli (“mackerel”), from brithelli, plural of brithel (“mackerel”), from brith (“spotted”)

Noun

brill (plural brills or brill)

  1. A type of flatfish, Scophthalmus rhombus.

Etymology 2

Abbreviation of brilliant.

Adjective

brill (comparative more brill, superlative most brill)

  1. (Britain, slang) Wonderful, amusing. Denotes approval of the noun it is applied to, comparable to "cool".
