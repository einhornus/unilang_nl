Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch pert, pāert, from Old Dutch *pered, from Late Latin paraverēdus. Compare German Pferd.

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">paard n (plural paarden, diminutive paardje n)

<ol>
  • A horse, Equus caballus or Equus ferus.
  • (chess) A knight.
  • (gymnastics) A pommel horse.
  • (nautical) A rope hanging beneath a spar or other horizontal beam, often fixed in place by other ropes.
  • (derogatory, offensive) An ugly woman.
