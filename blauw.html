blauw (1883)


Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch blau, from Old Dutch *blāo, from Proto-West Germanic *blāu, from Proto-Germanic *blēwaz.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /blɑu̯/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: blauw
  • Rhymes:

Adjective[edit]

blauw (comparative blauwer, superlative blauwst)

  1. blue
  2. visibly bruised, as in blauwe plek 'a bluish bruise'
  3. (slang) drunk

Noun[edit]

blauw n (plural blauwen, diminutive blauwtje n)

  1. The colour blue.
  2. Police (especially in the phrase: blauw op straat (“police on the street”))
