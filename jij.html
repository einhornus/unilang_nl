jij (31)


Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch ji, northern form of gi, from Old Dutch gī, from Proto-Germanic *jīz, a northwest Germanic variant of *jūz, from Proto-Indo-European *yū. Doublet of gij.

Cognate with Low German ji, jie, English ye, West Frisian jimme, German ihr. See also gij, u.
