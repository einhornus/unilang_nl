kaddish (82322)



Etymology

From Hebrew קדיש‎ (kadísh, “kaddish”), from Jewish Aramaic קדיש‎ (qaddish, “holy”)

Pronunciation

  • (Uk) IPA(key): /ˈkadɪʃ/
  • (Us) IPA(key): /ˈkɑdɪʃ/

Noun

kaddish (plural kaddishes or kaddishim)

  1. A Jewish prayer recited during services, and when mourning the death of a close relative.
