joekel (18984)


Etymology

Borrowed from Romani rom">ʒukel (“dog”), from Persian جاهل‎ (jâhel, “foolish, ignorant”), from Arabic جاهِل‎ (jāhil, “ignorant”). The sense development from dog to large entity was mediated by the sense large dog.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈju.kəl/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: joe‧kel

Noun

joekel m (plural joekels, diminutive joekeltje n)

  1. whopper (something remarkably large)
    De tweet staat vol met zulke joekels van fouten dat het niet te geloven is. ― The tweet is full of such whoppers of errors that it is unbelievable.
  2. (archaic, slang) dog
    Synonym: hond
