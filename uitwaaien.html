uitwaaien (72245)



Etymology

From uit- (“out-”) + waaien (“to blow”, of wind).

Pronunciation

  • Audio
    		(file)

Verb

uitwaaien

  1. (idiomatic) to go out in windy weather, particularly into nature or a park, as a means of refreshing oneself and clearing one's mind
