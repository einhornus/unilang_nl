Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch aenslaen. Equivalent to aan +‎ slaan.

Pronunciation[edit]

Verb[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">aanslaan

<ol>
  • (transitive) to strike physically so as to activate or produce an effect
  • (transitive) to tax (a taxable person) with a tax assessment
  • (intransitive) to start, to kick in, to switch on
  • (intransitive, of a dog) to start barking (as a sign of alarm)
  • (intransitive) to succeed, to have an effect
  • (intransitive) to catch on, to become popular
    Constant leest daar een vertaling van zijn manifest voor, dat echter helemaal niet aanslaat bij het publiek.
    Constant reads aloud a translation of his manifest there, which however does not at all catch on with the audience.
