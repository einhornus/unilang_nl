finch (8543)

<br>

Etymology[edit]

From Middle English fynche, from Old English finċ, from Proto-Germanic *finkiz (compare Dutch vink, German Fink), from Proto-Indo-European *(s)pingos (“chaffinch”). Compare Welsh pinc (“finch”), Ancient Greek σπίγγος (spíngos, “chaffinch”), Russian пенка (pénka, “wren”), Sanskrit फिङ्गक (phiṅgaka, “drongo, shrike”).
