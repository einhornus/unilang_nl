Etymology 1[edit]

See zijn. The infinitive wezen (from Proto-Germanic *wesaną) is the original infinitive of the verb but has fallen into marginal use, typically in an informal register or in dialects.

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">wezen n (plural wezens, diminutive wezentje n)

<ol>
  • A being, creature
  • (as the second part of a compound noun) system, sector, institution (in an abstract sense)
  • (used absolutely, with definite article or possessive determiner) The essence of something
    Zijn vloek had haar in haar diepste wezen geraakt. ― His curse had hit her in her deepest core.
