veulen (20911)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch volen, from Old Dutch">Old Dutch *folīn, from Proto-Germanic *fulīną, from *fulô, cognate with English language">English foal, German Fohlen.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈvøːlə(n)/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: veu‧len
  • Rhymes: -øːlən

Noun

veulen n (plural veulens, diminutive veulentje n)

  1. A foal, the young of any equine and some other quadrupeds, either colt (male) or filly (female); unless specified, usually a horse's
  2. (figuratively) A young person, especially behaving naively; fool
