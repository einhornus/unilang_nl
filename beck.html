Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle English bek, bekk, becc, from Old Norse bekkr (“a stream or brook”), from Proto-Germanic *bakiz (“stream”). Cognate with Low German bek, beck, German Bach, Dutch beek, Old English bæc, bec, bæċe, beċe (“beck, brook”). Doublet of batch. More at beach.

    Etymology 2[edit]

    From Middle English bekken, a shortened form of Middle English bekenen, from Old English bēcnan, bēacnian (“to signify; beckon”), from Proto-Germanic *baukną (“beacon”). More at beacon.
