bronchitis (38466)

<br>

Etymology[edit]

From Late Latin bronchus, from Ancient Greek βρόγχος (brónkhos, “windpipe”) +‎ -itis.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Uk) IPA(key): /bɹɒŋˈkaɪtɪs/

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">bronchitis (countable and uncountable, plural bronchitises or bronchitides)

<ol>
  • (uncountable) An inflammation of the bronchi of the lungs, that causes the cilia of the bronchial epithelial cells to stop functioning.
  • (countable) An occurrence of, a case (patient) of, or a type of bronchitis.

    • Zelfstandig naamwoord

    bronchitis

    <ol>
  • (medisch) ontsteking van de luchtpijptak
    • Rokende ouders bezorgen hun kinderen bronchitis, astma en sinusitis, dat staat spijkerhard vast.
