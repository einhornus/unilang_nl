Etymology 1

Borrowed from German Rakete, from Italian it">rocchetta, from it">rochetto (“rocket”, literally “a bobbin”), diminutive of it">rocca (“a distaff”), of Germanic origin, perhaps Gothic language">Gothic got">rukka, from Proto-Germanic *rukkô (“a distaff, a staff with flax fibres tied loosely to it, used in spinning thread”). Cognate with Old High German">Old High German goh">rocco, goh">rocko, goh">roccho, goh">rocho ("a distaff"; > German Rocken (“a distaff”)), Swedish rock (“a distaff”), Icelandic is">rokkur (“a distaff”), English">Middle English rocke (“a distaff”). More at rock⁴.

Noun

raket f or m (plural raketten, diminutive raketje n)

  1. rocket, missile
  2. firework
  3. a type of ice cream in the shape of a rocket

Etymology 2

Borrowed from Middle French roquette, from Italian it">ruchetta, diminutive of it">ruca, from Latin eruca. Cognate to arugula.

Noun

  1. (botany) a rocket (type of plant)

Etymology 3
