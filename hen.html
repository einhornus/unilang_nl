    Etymology 2[edit]

    From Middle Dutch henne, from Old Dutch *henna, from Proto-Germanic *hanjō, from Proto-Indo-European *keh₂n- (“to sing”).

    Noun[edit]

    <strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">hen f (plural hennen, diminutive hennetje n, masculine haan)

    <ol>
  • hen, female chicken; female of a related species.
  • A female of the species of birds brooding on the ground.
  • (figuratively) 'bird', colloquial term for a human female
    • Synonyms[edit]
    • (female chicken) kip, kieken
    • (brooding bird) kloek
    • (female human; informal) kippetje, duifje
    Related terms[edit]
    • haan m (rooster)
    • hoen, hoender n (fowl)
