Etymology 1

A contraction of veder, from Middle Dutch vedere, from Old Dutch">Old Dutch fethara, from Proto-Germanic *feþrō, from Proto-Indo-European *péth₂r̥ ~ pth₂én- (“feather, wing”), from *peth₂- (“to fly”). The sense "spring" is derived from the ability of feathers to resume their shape when bent.

Cognate with Low German">Low German Fedder, German Feder, West Frisian language">West Frisian fear, English language">English feather, Danish da">fjer, Swedish fjäder.

Noun

veer f (plural veren, diminutive veertje n)

  1. feather
    Synonym: pluim
  2. spring (e.g. metallic helix which resists stress)
