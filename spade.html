Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch spade, from Old Dutch *spado, from Proto-Germanic *spadô.

Noun

spade m (plural spaden or spades)

Etymology 2

From Middle Dutch spâde, from Old Dutch *spādi, from Proto-Germanic *spēdi- (“late”), which is related to *spōaną (“to succeed”)[1]. Cognate with German spät (“late”).

Adjective

spade (comparative spader, superlative spaadst)

  1. (archaic) late
