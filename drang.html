drang (8264)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch dranc, from Old Dutch">Old Dutch *thrang, from Proto-Germanic *þrangwaz. Related to dringen (from Proto-Germanic *þrinhwaną). Cognate with English language">English throng, German Drang, Norwegian no">trang.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /drɑŋ/
  • Rhymes: -ɑŋ
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: drang

Noun

drang m (plural drangen, diminutive drangetje n)

  1. pressure
  2. urge, longing
  3. (archaic) throng, multitude, mass

Zelfstandig naamwoord

drang m

  1. innerlijke neiging om iets te doen
