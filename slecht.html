Etymology 1[edit]

From an earlier meaning of “simple, ordinary, inferior, plain”, from Middle Dutch slecht (“smooth, flat”), from Old Dutch *sleht, from Proto-Germanic *slihtaz.

Adjective[edit]

slecht (comparative slechter, superlative slechtst)

  1. bad
  2. (dialectal, obsolete) ordinary, simple, common, mean

Etymology 2[edit]

Verb[edit]

slecht

  1. first-, second- and third-person singular present indicative of slechten
  2. imperative of slechten
