bliss (33512)


Etymology

From Middle English bliss, from Old English bliss, variant of earlier blīds, blīþs (“joy, gladness”), from Proto-West Germanic *blīþisi (“joy, goodness, kindness”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /blɪs/
  • Rhymes: -ɪs

Noun

bliss (countable and uncountable, plural blisses)

  1. perfect happiness
