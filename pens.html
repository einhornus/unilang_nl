Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch pense, from Old French panse (“belly; tripe”), from Latin pantex (“belly; guts”). Cognate with English paunch.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /pɛns/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: pens
  • Rhymes:

Noun[edit]

pens f (plural pensen, diminutive pensje n)

  1. paunch, rumen
  2. tripe
  3. (cooking) tripe filled with minced meat
  4. potbelly
  5. (dialectal) stomach

Synonyms[edit]

  • (paunch): rumen
  • (potbelly): bierbuik, buikje, spekbuik

  3. (informeel) buik
