drive (16557)


Etymology 1[edit]

From Old Norse drífa, from Proto-Germanic *drībaną, cognate with Swedish driva, English drive, Dutch drijven, German treiben.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈd̥ʁiːʋə], [ˈd̥ʁiːʊ]

Verb[edit]

drive (imperative driv, present driver, past drev, past participle drevet, c dreven, definite and plural drevne)

  1. drive
  2. run
  3. force
  4. power
  5. drift

Etymology 2[edit]

From Old Norse drífa f, derived form the verb.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈd̥ʁiːʋə], [ˈd̥ʁiːʊ]

Noun[edit]

drive c (singular definite driven, plural indefinite driver)

  1. drift

Etymology 3[edit]

From English drive.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): [ˈd̥ɹɑjʋ]

Noun[edit]

drive c (singular definite driven, not used in plural form)

  1. drive (psychology: desire or interest, self-motivation)

Noun[edit]

drive n (singular definite drivet, plural indefinite drives)

  1. drive (golf: stroke made with a driver)
