Etymology 1

From Middle English nyghtyngale, nightingale, niȝtingale, alteration (with intrusive n) of nyghtgale, nightegale, from Old English ang">nihtegala, ang">nihtegale (“nightingale; night-raven”, literally “night-singer”), from Proto-Germanic *nahtigalǭ (“nightingale”), equivalent to night +‎ gale. Cognate with Saterland Frisian Noachtegoal (“nightingale”), Dutch nachtegaal (“nightingale”), German Low German Nachtigall (“nightingale”), German Nachtigall (“nightingale”), Danish nattergal (“nightingale”), Swedish näktergal (“nightingale”), Icelandic næturgali (“nightingale”).

Noun

nightingale (plural nightingales)

  1. A European songbird, Luscinia megarhynchos, of the family Muscicapidae.
