onder (251)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch onder, from Old Dutch under, from Proto-Germanic *under. Cognate with Saxon">Old Saxon undar, German unter, English under.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈɔnˌdər/
  • audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: on‧der
  • Rhymes: -ɔndər

Adverb

onder

  1. under, downwards
  2. (postpositional) (to) under (implying motion).
    Hij ging zo de auto onder.
    It went under the car just like that.
Related terms
  • onder-

Preposition

onder

  1. under, below, beneath, underneath
  2. among, including
    Hij is onder ons!
    He is among us!
