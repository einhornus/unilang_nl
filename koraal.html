Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch corael, from Old French coral, from Latin corallium, from Ancient Greek κοραλλιών (koralliṓn); see coral for more.

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">koraal n (plural koralen, diminutive koraaltje n)

<ol>
  • A coral; any of various coralline tiny organisms of the class Anthozoa, notably of genus Corallium, who live in marine colonies.
  • The coral (calceous substance) they produce while building their colonies.
    • Synonyms[edit]
    • (organism): koraaldier, koraalpoliep
    Related terms[edit]
    • koralijn n

    Noun[edit]

    <strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">koraal f (plural koralen, diminutive koraaltje n)
