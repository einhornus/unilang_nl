Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch perceel, from older parcheel, from Old French parcelle, from Vulgar Latin *particella, from Latin particula.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /pɛrˈseːl/
  • Audio

  • Rhymes:

Noun[edit]

perceel n (plural percelen, diminutive perceeltje n)

  1. A parcel of land, a plot.
    Synonyms: kavel, kaveling
  2. building premises
  3. (uncommon, possibly dated or archaic) A building.
  4. A lot in an auction.
