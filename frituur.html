Etymology 1[edit]

Borrowed from Middle French friture, from Latin frīctūra, from frīctūrus.

Noun[edit]

frituur f (plural frituren, diminutive frituurtje n) (Belgium)

  1. Deep-fried food, now especially used of snacks and fast food. [from 16th c.]
    We eten morgenochtend frituur.
    We will eat deep-fried fastfood tomorrow morning.
  2. A chip shop, a friterie, a fast food takeaway selling deep-fried food. [from 20th c.]
Synonyms[edit]

(chip shop):

  • frietkot (Belgium)
  • friettent
  • snackbar
