koffer (1841)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch cofer, couer, from Old French cofre, coffre, from Latin cophinus (“basket”), from Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek κόφινος (kóphinos, “basket”).

  • Rhymes: -ɔfər

Noun

koffer m (plural koffers, diminutive koffertje n)

  1. A suitcase.
  2. A luggage compartment in a small vehicle; a trunk, a boot.
  3. (now Suriname) A trunk, a large chest used to move luggage.
    Synonyms: hutkoffer, reiskist
