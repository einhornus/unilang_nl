handhaven (8652)

<br>

Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch hanthaven, from Middle High German hanthaben, itself a calque from Old French maintenir, from Vulgar Latin manūteneō. Likely based also on Middle Dutch handhave (“handle”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɦɑntˌɦaː.və(n)/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: hand‧ha‧ven

Verb[edit]

handhaven

  1. (transitive) to enforce, to maintain, to uphold
  2. (transitive) to persevere with, to persist with/in
  3. (reflexive) to remain in charge, to remain in place
