semester (6857)


Etymology

From German Semester, from New Latin">New Latin sēmestris ("lasting six months"), from sex (“six”) + mēnsis (“month”).

Pronunciation

  • (Received Pronunciation) IPA(key): /sɪˈmɛstə/
  • (General American">General American) EnPR: sĭ-mĕstʹər, IPA(key): /sɪˈmɛstɚ/
  • Hyphenation: se‧mes‧ter
  • Rhymes: -ɛstə(ɹ)

Noun

semester (plural semesters)

  1. Half of a school year (United States">Us) or academic year such as fall or spring semester.
    I will graduate at the end of the spring semester.
  2. A period or term of six months.
