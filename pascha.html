pascha (31653)

<br>

Etymology[edit]

From Ancient Greek πάσχα (páskha, “Passover”), from Aramaic פסחא‎ (paskha), from Hebrew פסח‎ (pésakh).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈpas.kʰa/
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈpas.ka/

Noun[edit]

pascha f (genitive paschae); first declension<br /> pascha n (variously declined, genitive paschae or paschatis); first declension, third declension

  1. Pascha / Passover or Easter
  2. the Paschal Lamb
