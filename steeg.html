Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch steech, stege, from Old Dutch">Old Dutch steyga, *stega (“stairway”), from Proto-Germanic *stigǭ (“staircase; ladder”). Cognate with Low German">Middle Low German stēge, German German Steg.

Noun

steeg f (plural stegen, diminutive steegje n)

  1. An alleyway; a lane (between walls, fencing, etc.); a bystreet.
    Ze wachtten hem op in een steegje.
    They awaited him in an alley.
