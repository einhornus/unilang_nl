schallen (50776)



Etymology

From Middle Dutch scellen (“loud sound”), from Old Dutch skellan, from Proto-Germanic *skellan-, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *kelh₁- (“to shout”).[1][2]

Cognate with Old English scellan (“to sound, make a noise”) and German schallen (“to sound, to resound”). Also see Old Norse skjalla (“to clatter”), Old English hlowan.

Verb

schallen

  1. to sound loudly
  2. to resound, to echo
