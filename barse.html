Etymology 1

From Middle English bars, from Old English bærs (“a fish, perch”), from Proto-Germanic *barsaz (“perch”, literally “prickly”). Cognate with Dutch baars (“perch, bass”), German Barsch (“perch”).

Noun

barse (plural barses)

  1. The perch; any of various marine and freshwater fish resembling the perch.
Related terms
  • bass
