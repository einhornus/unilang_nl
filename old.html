old (20263)


Etymology

From Middle Low German ôlt, from Saxon">Old Saxon ald, from Proto-Germanic *aldaz. The A became an O through the effect of the velarised L in the same manner as in Dutch oud. Cognate with English old, Dutch oud, German alt, West Frisian âld. Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *h₂eltós (“grow, nourish”), from *h₂el- (“grow, nourish”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ɔːɫt/
