aspen (15574)


Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈæspən/

Etymology 1

From Middle English aspen, corresponding to asp +‎ -en.

Adjective

aspen (comparative more aspen, superlative most aspen)

  1. Pertaining to the asp or aspen tree.
  2. (obsolete) Tremulous, trembling.
    • a. 1631, John Donne">John Donne, ‘The Apparition’, Poems (1633):
      And then poore Aspen wretch, neglected thou / Bath'd in a cold quicksilver sweat wilt lye / A veryer ghost than I […].
  3. (obsolete) Of a woman's tongue: wagging, gossiping.

Etymology 2

From Middle English aspen, from Old English ang">æspen, Old English ang">æspan (combining form), from Old English ang">æspe (“aspen”). More at asp.

Noun

aspen (plural aspens)

  1. The asp tree; a kind of poplar tree (genus Populus sect. Populus). A medium-size tree with thin, straight trunks of a greenish-white color.
    Above a certain elevation, the aspens gave way to scrubby, gnarled pines.
    • 1912 January, Zane Grey">Zane Grey, chapter 8, in the Purple Sage">Riders of the Purple Sage: A Novel, New York, N.Y.; London: Harper (publisher)">Harper & Brothers Publishers, OCLC 6868219:
      Instead there were the white of aspens, streaks of branch and slender trunk glistening from the green of leaves [the darker green of oaks, and through the middle of this forest, from wall to wall, ran a winding line of brilliant green which marked the course of cottonwoods and willows">…] .
  2. (uncountable) The wood of such a tree; usually pale, lightweight and soft.
    She claimed that aspen was the only "proper" material from which make a wicker basket.
