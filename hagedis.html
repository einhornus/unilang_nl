hagedis (10869)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch hagetisse, from Old Dutch *egithassa, from Proto-Germanic *agiþahsijǭ (“lizard”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˌɦaːɣəˈdɪs/
  • audio

  • Rhymes: -ɪs

Noun

hagedis m (plural hagedissen, diminutive hagedisje n)

  1. lizard, non-snake reptilian of the subclass Squamata, especially of the Lacertilia
