Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch lêet, from Old Dutch *lēth, from Proto-Germanic *laiþą.

Noun[edit]

leed n (uncountable)

  1. grief, sorrow
  2. harm

Etymology 2[edit]

From Middle Dutch lêet, from Old Dutch lēth, from Proto-Germanic *laiþaz.

Adjective[edit]

leed (comparative leder, superlative leedst)

  1. (Belgium) angry
  2. sad
