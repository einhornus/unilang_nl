wo (13321)


Etymology 1

From Middle Low German wô (“how”), from Saxon">Old Saxon [Term?], from Proto-Germanic *hwō. Cognate with English how, German wie, Dutch hoe.

Pronunciation

  • (in some dialects) IPA(key): /vɔu̯/
  • (traditional) IPA(key): [wɔʊ̯]

Adverb

wo

  1. how
    Wo vele Daag?
    How many days?

Etymology 2

From Saxon">Old Saxon hwē, from Proto-Germanic *hwaz. Compare English who, whom, whose.

Pronoun

wo

  1. (dialect">Low Prussian, relative) who, which
    (dialect">Low Prussian)
    Dat, wo ös... ― that which is...

Usage notes

The dative form (also used for the accusative) is woom (wom); the genitive form is woos (wos).

Zelfstandig naamwoord

wo m

  1. (afkorting), (tijdrekening), (dag) woensdag, de derde dag van de werkweek
    «Open: di, wo, do, vr; dicht: za, zo, ma.»
    Geopend op dinsdag, woensdag, donderdag en vrijdag; gesloten op zaterdag, zondag en maandag.
