wo (13321)


Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Low German wô (“how”), from Old Saxon [Term?], from Proto-Germanic *hwō. Cognate with English how, German wie, Dutch hoe.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (in some dialects) IPA(key): /vɔu̯/
  • (traditional) IPA(key): [wɔʊ̯]

Adverb[edit]

wo

  1. how
    Wo vele Daag?
    How many days?

Etymology 2[edit]

From Old Saxon hwē, from Proto-Germanic *hwaz. Compare English who, whom, whose.

Pronoun[edit]

wo

  1. (Low Prussian, relative) who, which
    (Low Prussian)
    Dat, wo ös... ― that which is...

Usage notes[edit]

The dative form (also used for the accusative) is woom (wom); the genitive form is woos (wos).

Zelfstandig naamwoord

wo

  1. (afkorting), (tijdrekening), (dag) woensdag, de derde dag van de werkweek
    «Open: di, wo, do, vr; dicht: za, zo, ma.»
    Geopend op dinsdag, woensdag, donderdag en vrijdag; gesloten op zaterdag, zondag en maandag.
