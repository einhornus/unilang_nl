coup (15188)


Etymology

Borrowed from French coup, from Middle French [Term?], from Old French">Old French colp, from Vulgar Latin *colpus, from Latin colaphus, from Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek κόλαφος (kólaphos).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): (chiefly Netherlands) /kup/, (chiefly Belgium) /ku/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: coup
  • Rhymes: -up, -u
  • Homophones: koe, coupe

Noun

coup m (plural coups, diminutive coupje n)

  1. A coup, a coup d'état, a putsch.
    Synonyms: putsch, staatsgreep
  2. A coup, a quick, surprising, brilliant move or action.
