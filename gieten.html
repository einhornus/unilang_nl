gieten (15170)


Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch gieten, from Old Dutch gietan, from Proto-Germanic *geutaną, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰewd- (“to pour”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɣi.tə(n)/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: gie‧ten
  • Rhymes:

Verb[edit]

gieten

  1. (transitive) to pour
  2. (transitive) to mould or cast
  3. (impersonal, of rain) to pour down, to precipitate heavily
    Synonym: plenzen
