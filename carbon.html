Etymology 1[edit]

Probably borrowed from French carbone, ultimately from Latin carbō. The sense “fibre-reinforced polymer” derived from English carbon.

Noun[edit]

carbon n (uncountable, diminutive carbonnetje n)

  1. fibre-reinforced polymer
  2. black diamond

Etymology 2[edit]

From carbonpapier.
