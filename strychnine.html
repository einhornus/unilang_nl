strychnine (32996)



Etymology

From French strychnine, from Greek στρύχνος (strýchnos, “sleepy nightshade”) + -ine.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈstɹɪk.niːn/

Noun

strychnine (usually uncountable, plural strychnines)

  1. (organic chemistry) A very toxic, colourless crystalline alkaloid, derived from nux vomica, used as a pesticide

Zelfstandig naamwoord

strychnine v / m [3]

  1. (plantkunde) zeer vergiftig alkaloïde vooral gewonnen uit de braaknoot (Strychnos nux-vomica ) met de formule: C21H22N2O2
