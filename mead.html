mead (30389)


Pronunciation

  • Audio (US)
    		(file)
  • Rhymes: -iːd
  • Homophone: meed

Etymology 1

From Middle English mede, from Old English ang">medu, from Proto-Germanic *meduz, from Proto-Indo-European *médʰu (“honey; honey wine”).

Noun

mead (usually uncountable, plural meads)

  1. An alcoholic drink fermented from honey and water.
  2. (United States">US) A drink composed of syrup of sarsaparilla or other flavouring extract, and water, and sometimes charged with carbon dioxide.

Related terms

  • bragget (“drink made from ale, honey & spices”)
