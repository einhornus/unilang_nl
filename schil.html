Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch schelle, schille, from Old Dutch *skella, from Proto-West Germanic *skallju, from Proto-Germanic *skaljō. Cognate with Middle Low German schelle, English shell, Old Norse skel, Gothic 𐍃𐌺𐌰𐌻𐌾𐌰 (skalja).

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">schil f or m (plural schillen, diminutive schilletje n)

<ol>
  • peel, rind, skin, bark
  • crust (soil)
  • shell (egg or mollusc)
  • (obsolete) slice
  • (obsolete) membrane covering the eye; used figuratively in the absolute plural (de schillen) for (mental) blindness
