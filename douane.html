Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from French douane, from Middle French doane, from Old French <small>[Term?], from Medieval Latin doana, dovana, from Andalusian Arabic *دُوَان‎ (*duwān), from earlier Arabic دِيوَان‎ (dīwān), from Persian دیوان‎ (dīwān, “customs, divan”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /duˈaːnə/
  • Audio

Noun[edit]

douane f (plural douanen or douanes)

  1. customs (service/authorities/administration)
  2. customs officer
    Synonyms: douanebeambte, douanier

Related terms[edit]

  • divan
  • douanier
