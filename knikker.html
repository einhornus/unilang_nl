knikker (19180)

<br>

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈknɪkər/
  • Rhymes:

Etymology 1[edit]

From knikken, from Proto-Germanic *knikkoną (“to fold, snap”), which is imitative.

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">knikker m (plural knikkers, diminutive knikkertje n)

<ol>
  • a marble (toy)
  • various similarly shaped objects, including: <ol>
  • a billiards ball
  • certain seeds
  • (humorous) a head, especially a bald skull (typically in kale knikker)
  • (by extension) a lump or stone
  • (metonymically) (in the plural, absolutely used with definite article: de knikkers) The stakes of a game, deal etc.
    • Synonyms[edit]
    • (marbles): marbel m, stuiter m

    Etymology 2[edit]

    Germanic: the root of knikken 'to nod' + -er
