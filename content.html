Etymology 1

From Middle French content (“satisfied”), from Latin contentus (“contained; satisfied”), past participle of continēre (“to contain”).

Etymology 2

From Middle English content (plural contentes, contence), from Latin contentus, past participle of continēre (“to hold in, contain”), as Etymology 1, above. English apparently developed a substantive form of the adjective, which is not mirrored in other Romance languages.
