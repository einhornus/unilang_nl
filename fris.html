Etymology 1

From late Middle Dutch frisch, vrisch, borrowed from High German">Middle High German vrisch, from High German">Old High German goh">frisc, from Proto-West Germanic *frisk, from Proto-Germanic *friskaz. Doublet of vers.

Adjective

fris (comparative frisser, superlative meest fris or frist)

  1. fresh, refreshing
  2. chilly
