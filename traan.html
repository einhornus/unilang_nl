Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch trâen, from Old Dutch trān, from Proto-Germanic *trahnuz. Cognate with German Träne (“tear”) and Tran (“fish oil”).

Noun[edit]

traan m or f (plural tranen, diminutive traantje n)

  1. tear (in the eye)

Noun[edit]

traan m or f (uncountable)

  1. fish oil
