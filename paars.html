paars (9300)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch paers, peers, pers (“purple”), from French pers (“blue-grey, dark violet”), from Medieval Latin persus, persum (“dark blue”). Possibly derived from either Latin Persae (“Persians”), Persia (“Persia”), referring to the colour of a garment, or from Late Latin">Late Latin persica (“peach”), referring to the colour of peach blossom.[1]

Adjective

paars (comparative paarser, superlative meest paars or paarst)

  1. purple
  2. (politics) relating to cooperation between liberals and social democrats of the third way [from late 20th c.]
