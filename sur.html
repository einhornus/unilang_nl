sur (40115)

Etymology[edit]

From Old Norse súrr, from Proto-Germanic *sūraz, from Proto-Indo-European *sūr-. Cognate with Danish sur, Icelandic súr, Dutch zuur, English sour and German sauer.

Adjective[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nn">sur (neuter singular surt, definite singular and plural sure, comparative surare, indefinite superlative surast, definite superlative suraste)

<ol>
  • sour
  • acidic
    sur nedbør ― rain
  • bad-tempered, annoyed, in a foul mood
