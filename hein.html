hein (22232)

<br>

Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch hein, from the verb heinen (“to physically demarcate lands”) (modern heinen, also omheinen).

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">hein m (plural heinen, diminutive heintje n)

<ol>
  • physical demarcation between fields or yards, like a fence, wall or ditch.
    • Related terms[edit]
    • haag, heg, hagen, behagen, omhagen
    • heinen, heining, omheinen, omheining, ijzerenheinig
