morrow (43386)


Etymology

From Middle English morwe, variant of morwen, from Old English ang">morgen, from Proto-Germanic *murganaz, *murginaz; compare Dutch morgen and German Morgen. See also the related morn, from the same Old English origin.

Noun

morrow (plural morrows)

  1. (archaic or poetic) The next or following day.
  2. (archaic) Morning.
