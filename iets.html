iets (73)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch iet, from Middle Dutch iewet, a contraction of Old Dutch iowiht. The Middle Dutch word is formed out of the articles Old Dutch ie- meaning 'anything', and a second article corresponding with Middle Dutch wicht, 'creature', of which its original meaning likely has been 'thing'. Cognate with Limburgish get, Central Franconian language">Central Franconian jet, English aught.

In the word, the suffix -s has been shaped under influence of the older Old Dutch niets wherein -s is the ending of an old partitive genitive.
