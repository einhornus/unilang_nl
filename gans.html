Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch gans (alongside gōs, gās), from Proto-Germanic *gans, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰans-. Compare Low German Gans, Goos, German Gans, West Frisian goes, English goose, Danish gås.

Noun[edit]

Indische gans<br />Bar-headed goose (literally: Indian goose)

gans f (plural ganzen, diminutive gansje n)

  1. A goose, a waterfowl of the family Anatidae, especially of the subfamily Anserinae excepting the genera Cygnus and Coscoroba; in non-scientific usage the term may be applied to larger waterfowl of the Anatidae who do not belong to the Anserinae.

Etymology 2[edit]

From Middle Dutch gansch, gands, gants, gans, from Old High German ganz, from Proto-Germanic *gantaz (“all, whole, entire”).

Determiner[edit]

gans

  1. whole, entire
    Het ganse land.
    The entire country.
