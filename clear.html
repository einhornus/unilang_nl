clear (26745)


Etymology

From Middle English clere, from Anglo-Norman cler, from Old French cler (Modern French clair), from Latin clarus. Displaced native Middle English schir (“clear, pure”) (from Old English ang">scīr (“clear, bright”)), Middle English skere (“clear, sheer”) (from Old English ang">scǣre and Old Norse non">skǣr (“sheer, clear, pure”)), Middle English smolt (“clear (of mind), serene”) (from Old English ang">smolt (“peaceful, serene”)). Cognate with Danish klar, Dutch klaar, French clair, German klar, Italian chiaro, Norwegian klar, Portuguese claro, Romanian clar, Spanish language">Spanish claro, and Swedish klar.
