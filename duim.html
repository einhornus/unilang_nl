Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch dume, from Old Dutch *thūmo, from Proto-Germanic *þūmô. Compare West Frisian tomme, Low German Dumen, German Daumen, English thumb, Danish tomme, Swedish tumme.

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">duim m (plural duimen, diminutive duimpje n)

<ol>
  • thumb, the inner-most finger
  • inch
  • pintle (jointed pivot of a hinge, consisting of the pin and the non-moving leaf)
