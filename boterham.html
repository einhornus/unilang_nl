boterham (6865)


Etymology

Alteration of earlier boteram, boterram, of uncertain origin. Possibly from boter (“butter”) + *ramme, *remme (“thick slice of bread”) (compare obsolete rammel, remmel (“thick slice of bread”) and West Flemish">West Flemish rammeke (“toasted sandwich”)). Alternatively perhaps from boter (“butter”) +‎ *ham (“chunk”).[1] Alternatively, short for boterenbroot (“buttered bread”). Unrelated to ham (“ham”). Related to Low German">Low German boteram and French bouterame, which are borrowed from the Dutch.
