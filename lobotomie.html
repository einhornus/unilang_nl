lobotomie (25272)



Etymology

Borrowed from English lobotomy, coined by Walter Jackson Freeman Ii, from Ancient Greek λοβός (lobós, “lobe (of the brain)”) + τομή (tomḗ, “cut, slice”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˌloː.boː.toːˈmi/
  • Audio

  • Hyphenation: lo‧bo‧to‧mie
  • Rhymes: -i
