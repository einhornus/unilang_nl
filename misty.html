misty (28077)


Etymology

From Middle English misty, mysty, misti, from Old English ang">mistiġ (“misty, dark”), equivalent to mist +‎ -y. Cognate with Scots misty, mistie (“misty”), Dutch mistig (“misty, foggy”), Middle Low German mistich (“foggy”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈmɪsti/
  • Rhymes: -ɪsti

Adjective

misty (comparative mistier, superlative mistiest)

  1. Covered in mist; foggy.
    It's very misty this morning; I can't see a thing!
  2. (figuratively) Dim; vague; obscure.
    a misty memory of his childhood
  3. (figuratively) With tears in the eyes; dewy-eyed.
    Her eyes grew misty the night her long-time friend passed away.

Related terms

  • mistify
