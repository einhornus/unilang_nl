  • Rhymes: -ɔfəl

Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch schoffel (“shovel”), from Old Dutch [Term?], from Proto-Germanic *skuflō.

Noun

schoffel m (plural schoffels, diminutive schoffeltje n)

  1. hoe (specifically a Dutch hoe)
  2. (dialectal, northern and eastern Netherlands) shovel
    Synonyms: schep, schop, spade

Etymology 2

See the etymology of the main entry.

Verb

schoffel

  1. first-person singular present indicative of schoffelen
  2. imperative of schoffelen
