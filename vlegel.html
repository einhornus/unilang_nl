Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch vlegel, vleil, from Old Dutch *flegil, from Latin flagellum (“whipping flail”).

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">vlegel m (plural vlegels, diminutive vlegeltje n)

<ol>
  • A flail, whipping implement; also in figurative sense
  • A flail, threshing tool (or by extension equivalent machinery)
  • (derogatory) A rogue, imp, rascal, scamp
    • Synonyms[edit]
    • (whipping flail) gesel
    • (rogue) rekel m, schelm m, vlegelsman m
