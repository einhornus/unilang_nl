vlegel (26403)


Pronunciation

  • Rhymes: -eːɣəl

Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch vlegel, vleil, from Old Dutch *flegil, from Latin flagellum (“whipping flail”).

Noun

vlegel m (plural vlegels, diminutive vlegeltje n)

  1. A flail, whipping implement; also in figurative sense
  2. A flail, threshing tool (or by extension equivalent machinery)
  3. (derogatory) A rogue, imp, rascal, scamp
Synonyms
  • (whipping flail) gesel
  • (rogue) rekel m, schelm m, vlegelsman m

Etymology 2

Noun

vlegel m (plural vlegels, diminutive vlegeltje n)

  1. Alternative form of vleugel

Etymology 3

Verb

vlegel

  1. first-person singular present indicative of vlegelen
  2. imperative of vlegelen
