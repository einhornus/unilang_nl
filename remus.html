remus (44763)

<br>

Etymology[edit]

From a Proto-Indo-European root *h₁reh₁- shared with Ancient Greek ἐρετμός (eretmós, “oar”), ἐρέτης (erétēs, “rower”), τριήρης (triḗrēs, “trireme”), and possibly English oar.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈreː.mus/, [ˈreː.mʊs]
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈre.mus/, [ˈrɛː.mus]

Noun[edit]

rēmus m (genitive rēmī); second declension

  1. oar
