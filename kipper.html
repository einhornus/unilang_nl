Etymology 1

Old English">Old English ang">cypera "male salmon," perhaps related to Old English">Old English ang">coper "reddish-brown metal" (see copper), on resemblance of color. Another theory connects it to kip "sharp, hooked lower jaw of the male salmon in breeding season," from English">Middle English kippen "to seize, snatch" but OED doubts this.

Noun

kipper (plural kippers)

  1. A split, salted and smoked herring or salmon.
  2. A male salmon after spawning.
  3. (military, Raf World War II code name) A patrol to protect fishing boats in the Irish and North Seas against attack from the air.
  4. (Great Britain">Britain, naval slang) A torpedo.
    • 2009, Jean Hood, Submarine (page 197)
      'Fancy running the risk of getting a kipper [a torpedo] to go with his grub.'
