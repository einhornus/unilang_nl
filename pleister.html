pleister (13160)


Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch plaester, from Old French plastre, from Latin plastrum, from emplastrum, from Ancient Greek ἔμπλαστρον (émplastron), from ἔμπλαστον (émplaston).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈplɛi.stər/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: pleis‧ter
  • Rhymes: -ɛistər

Noun[edit]

pleister f (plural pleisters, diminutive pleistertje n)

  1. plaster, sticking plaster, band-aid

Synonyms[edit]

  • (Belgium, informal) plakker
  • kleefpleister

Noun[edit]

pleister n (uncountable)

  1. plaster (lime mixture)

Synonyms[edit]

  • kalk, witkalk, muurkalk, plaaster, pleistermortel, pleisterkalk, stuc, witsel

Verb[edit]

pleister

  1. first-person singular present indicative of pleisteren
  2. imperative of pleisteren
