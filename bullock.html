bullock (15446)

<br>

Etymology[edit]

From Middle English bullok, from Old English bulluc, corresponding to bull +‎ -ock (diminutive suffix). Compare Middle Dutch boelekijn (“bullock”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈbʊlək/

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="en">bullock (plural bullocks)

<ol>
  • (archaic) A young bull.
    • 1611, The Holy Bible, […] (King James Version), London: […] Robert Barker, […], <small>OCLC 964384981, Leviticus 8:14:
      And he brought the bullock for the sin offering: and Aaron and his sons laid their hands upon the head of the bullock for the sin offering.
  • A castrated bull; an ox.
