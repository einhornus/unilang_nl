Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from New Latin rectum, abbreviation of Latin rectum intestinum (“the straight intestine”), rectum, neuter of rectus (“straight”). See right.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɹɛktəm/
  • Audio (Uk)
    		(file)

Noun[edit]

rectum (plural recta or rectums)

  1. (anatomy) The terminal part of the large intestine through which feces pass after exiting the colon.
