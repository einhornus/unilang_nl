hummus (51666)



Etymology

From Turkish humus or directly from Arabic حُمُّص‎ (ḥummuṣ).

Pronunciation

  • EnPR: ho͝o'məs, IPA(key): /ˈhʊməs/
  • EnPR: hŭ'məs, IPA(key): /ˈhʌməs/
  • EnPR: ho͞o'məs, IPA(key): /ˈhuːməs/

Noun

hummus (usually uncountable, plural hummuses)

  1. A Levantine Arab dip made of chickpea paste with various additions, such as olive oil, fresh garlic, lemon juice, and tahini, often eaten with pitta bread, or as a meze. It is mostly eaten in the Levant.

Zelfstandig naamwoord

hummus v / m

  1. (voeding) puree van o.m. gekookte kikkererwten, oorspronkelijk afkomstig uit het Midden-Oosten (Libanon)
Verwante begrippen
  • tahini, pitabrood
Synoniemen
  • humus
