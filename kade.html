kade (12452)


Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch cade, later also caey. A hypercorrection of kaai, as the original word was thought to have contained -d-, which tends to be replaced by -j- in many words. This etymology is incomplete. You can help Wiktionary by elaborating on the origins of this term.
