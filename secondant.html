secondant (38937)


Etymology

Borrowed from French secondant. Equivalent to seconderen +‎ -ant.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˌseː.kɔnˈdɑnt/, /sə.kɔnˈdɑnt/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: se‧con‧dant
  • Rhymes: -ɑnt

Noun

secondant m (plural secondanten, diminutive secondantje n)

  1. (formal) A second, a prominent aide or assistant, chiefly one who is left in charge when a superior is absent.
  2. A second (attendant of a duel or boxing match standing in for a contestant).
  3. (historical) A second teacher, one substituting a headteacher when necessary (chiefly used for former times when schools frequently had only one or a few teachers).
