Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch wāert, wēert, from Old Dutch *wert, from Proto-Germanic *werduz.[1]

Noun[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">waard m (plural waarden, diminutive waardje n, feminine waardin)

<ol>
  • innkeeper, publican
    Synonyms: herbergier, kastelein
    Hyponym: waardin
  • landlord, host
  • (obsolete) The master of the household; a husband
  • (obsolete) A protector, lord

    • Etymology 2[edit]
