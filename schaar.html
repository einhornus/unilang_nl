Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch scare, schere, from Old Dutch *skāri, originally a plural form.

Noun[edit]

schaar f (plural scharen, diminutive schaartje n)

  1. pair of scissors
  2. claw (e.g. of a crab)

Etymology 2[edit]

Noun[edit]

schaar f (plural scharen, diminutive schaartje n)

  1. Alternative form of schare.
