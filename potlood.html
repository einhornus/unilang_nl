potlood (7436)


Etymology

From pot (“pot”) +‎ lood (“lead”). Originally it was a name for graphite, used for glazing pots, which was misidentified as a form of lead. Compare English language">English black lead.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈpɔt.loːt/
  • audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: pot‧lood

Noun

potlood n (plural potloden, diminutive potloodje n)

  1. A pencil.
  2. Graphite.
    Synonym: grafiet
  3. (especially) Graphite powder.
