maple (40524)


Etymology

From Old English">Old English ang">*mapul (attested in ang">mapultrēow and ang">mapulder), from Proto-Germanic *mapulaz (compare Old Saxon">Old Saxon mapulder, Old High German">Old High German goh">mazaltra, goh">mazzaltra; Old Norse">Old Norse non">mǫpurr, Low German">Middle Low German mapeldorn, dialectal Dutch meppel, German Masseller, Maßholder), perhaps a blend of *masuraz (“knob; maple-tree”) (compare Old English">Old English ang">mæsen (“maple”), Old Norse">Old Norse non">mǫsurr (“maple”), German Maser (“knob, offshoot”)) and *aplaz (“apple”) (see apple), from *masą (“lump, knob”) (compare obsolete German Mase (“scar”), modern Maser (“speck, measle”).[1][2] More at measles.

Pronunciation

Noun

maple (countable and uncountable, plural maples)

  1. A tree of the Acer genus, characterised by its usually palmate leaves and winged seeds.
  2. The wood of such a tree, prized for its hardness and attractive appearance
