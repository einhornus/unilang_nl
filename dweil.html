dweil (16855)


Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /dʋɛi̯l/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: dweil
  • Rhymes: -ɛi̯l

Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch dwâle, *dweile, from Old Dutch">Old Dutch *thwāla, *thweila, from Proto-West Germanic *þwahilu. Doublet of dwaal.

Noun

dweil m (plural dweilen, diminutive dweiltje n)

  1. floorcloth, mop
  2. worthless person; weakling

Etymology 2

See the etymology of the main entry.

Verb

dweil

  1. first-person singular present indicative of dweilen
  2. imperative of dweilen
