brutus (8433)


Etymology

An Oscan loanword, from Proto-Indo-European *gʷréh₂us. Cognate with Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek βαρύς (barús), Persian fa">گران‎ (gerân) and Sanskrit sa">गुरु (gurú). See also Latin gravis.

Pronunciation

  • (Classical) IPA(key): /ˈbruː.tus/, [ˈbruː.t̪ʊs]
  • (Ecclesiastical) IPA(key): /ˈbru.tus/, [ˈbruː.t̪us]

Adjective

brūtus (feminine brūta, neuter brūtum); first/second-declension adjective

  1. heavy, unwieldy
  2. dull, stupid, insensible, unreasonable, irrational
