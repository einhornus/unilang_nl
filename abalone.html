abalone (61939)



Etymology

From American Spanish abulón, from an indigenous language of the Monterey Bay area such as Rumsen/Southern Ohlone aūlun (“red abalone”)[1][2]

Pink abalone.

Pronunciation

Noun

abalone (usually uncountable, plural abalones)

  1. (Canada, Us, Australia) An edible univalve mollusc of the genus Haliotis, having a shell lined with mother-of-pearl. [from mid-19th century][3]
  2. (Canada, Us, Australia) The meat of the aforementioned mollusc. [from mid-19th century]

Synonyms

  • (Uk) ear-shell, (Guernsey) ormer, (New Zealand) paua, pawa, (South Africa) perlemoen
