patio (25646)


Etymology

From Spanish language">Spanish patio, either from Old Occitan pro">patu or pro">pati (compare Occitan and Catalan pati), from Latin pactum (“pact, agreement, bargain”), or alternatively from Latin patere (“to lie open”).

Pronunciation

  • (Received Pronunciation) IPA(key): /ˈpæti.əʊ/
  • (American English">US) IPA(key): /ˈpætioʊ/
  • Audio (US)
    		(file)

Noun

patio (plural patios)

  1. A paved outside area, adjoining a house, used for dining or recreation.
  2. An inner courtyard typical of traditional houses in some regions of Spain.
