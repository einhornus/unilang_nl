cloud (16404)


Etymology

From Middle English cloud, cloude, clod, clud, clude, from Old English ang">clūd (“mass of stone, rock, boulder, hill”), from Proto-Germanic *klūtaz, *klutaz (“lump, mass, conglomeration”), from Proto-Indo-European *gel- (“to ball up, clench”).

Cognate with Scots clood, clud (“cloud”), Dutch kluit (“lump, mass, clod”), German Low German Kluut, Kluute (“lump, mass, ball”), German Kloß (“lump, ball, dumpling”), Danish klode (“sphere, orb, planet”), Swedish klot (“sphere, orb, ball, globe”), Icelandic klót (“knob on a sword's hilt”). Related to English clod, clot, clump, club. Displaced native Middle English wolken, wolkne from Old English ang">wolcen (whence Modern English welkin), the commonest Germanic word (compare Dutch wolk, German Wolke).
