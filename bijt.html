Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch bijt, from biten (Dutch bijten). Cognate with Middle Low German bīt, West Frisian byt.

Noun[edit]

bijt f (plural bijten, diminutive bijtje n)

  1. a man-made hole in ice (on the surface of a body of water)
