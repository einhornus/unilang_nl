garrison (23845)


Etymology

From Old French garison, guarison, from Frankish [Term?], ultimately of Germanic origin; compare guard, ward. Doublet of warison.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈɡæɹɪsən/
  • (Mary–marry–merry merger) IPA(key): /ˈɡɛɹɪsən/
  • Audio (US-merged)

Noun

garrison (plural garrisons)

  1. A permanent military post.
  2. The troops stationed at such a post.
  3. (allusive) Occupants.
    • 1913, Robert Barr (writer)">Robert Barr, chapter 4, in Lord Stranleigh Abroad‎[1]:
