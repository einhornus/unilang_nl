analyse (5934)


Etymology

Borrowed from French analyse, from Medieval Latin analysis, from Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek ἀνάλυσις (análusis), from ἀναλύω (analúō, “I unravel, investigate”), from ἀνά (aná, “on, up”) + λύω (lúō, “I loosen”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /aːnaːˈliːzə/
  • analyse
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: ana‧ly‧se
  • Rhymes: -iːzə

Noun

analyse f (plural analysen or analyses, diminutive analysetje n)

  1. analysis (action of taking something apart to study it)
  2. (mathematics) analysis (mathematical study of functions, sequences, series, limits, derivatives and integrals)

Related terms

  • analytisch
  • analist
