deugd (6576)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch dōghet, dōget, from Old Dutch *dugath, *dugeth, *dugith (“virtue”), from Proto-Germanic *dugunþō (“usefulness, virtue”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰewgʰ- (“to be ready, be sufficient”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /døːxt/
  • audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: deugd
  • Rhymes: -øːxt

Noun

deugd f (plural deugden)

  1. virtue
