Etymology 1

From Middle Dutch kieme, kyme. Although the word is found as Old Dutch kīmo, it was probably borrowed from Low German">Middle Low German kīme, from Old Saxon">Old Saxon kīmo because it did not undergo the regular change of -ī- to -ij- (so that *kijm would be expected). Possible influence of Latin cyma is also possible. Ultimately from Proto-Germanic *kīmô.

Noun

kiem f or m (plural kiemen, diminutive kiempje n)

  1. (biology) seed, sprout, germ
  2. (figuratively) beginning, birth
  3. (pathology) germ (pathogen)
  4. (mathematics) germ
