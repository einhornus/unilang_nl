Etymology[edit]

From Old High German sat, from Proto-Germanic *sadaz. Cognate with German satt, Dutch zat.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /zaːt/

Adjective[edit]

sat (masculine saten, neuter sat, comparative méi sat, superlative am saatsten)

  1. full, sated
    Ech sinn esou sat!
    I'm so full!
  2. drunk, inebriated
