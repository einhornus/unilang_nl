bacon (9656)


Etymology

1899, "thin, smoked lard", from English bacon, from Middle English bacon (“meat from the back and sides of a pig”), from Old French bacon, bacun (“ham, strip of lard”), from Frankish *bakkō, from Proto-Germanic *bakō, *baką, *bakaz (“back”), from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeg- (“back, buttocks; to vault, arch”). Cognate with High German">Old High German goh">bahho, goh">bacho (“back, ham, side of bacon”), Saxon">Old Saxon baco (“back”), Dutch bake (“side of bacon, ham”), Old English ang">bæc (“back”).
