Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch pîpe, from Old Dutch *pīpa, from Proto-Germanic *pīpǭ and Vulgar Latin *pipa, both from Latin pipō.

Noun[edit]

pijp f (plural pijpen, diminutive pijpje n)

  1. pipe
    Rookt u pijp?
    Do you smoke pipe?
  2. tube
    Die pijp is lek.
    That tube is leaking.
    Synonym: buis

See the etymology of the main entry.

Verb[edit]

pijp

  1. first-person singular present indicative of pijpen
  2. imperative of pijpen
