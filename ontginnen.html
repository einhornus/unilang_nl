Etymology[edit]

From Middle Dutch ontginnen. Equivalent to ont- +‎ ginnen. Cognate to Old English onginnan and Old Saxon andginnan.

Verb[edit]

<strong class="Latn headword" lang="nl">ontginnen

<ol>
  • (transitive, of land) to develop into agricultural land
  • (transitive, of land) to remove the topsoil from
  • (transitive, of mines and mineral resources) to excavate, to extract from the earth
  • (transitive, obsolete) to begin, to commence
