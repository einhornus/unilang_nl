secretaris (6995)


Etymology[edit]

From Medieval Latin sēcrētārius (“one entrusted with secrets”), from Latin sēcrētus (“private, secret”), perfect passive participle of sēcernō (“I separate; I part; I reject”), from sē- (“apart”) + cernō (“I separate, sift”).

Noun[edit]

secretaris m (plural secretarissen, diminutive secretarisje n, feminine secretaresse)

  1. secretary
  2. receptionist
  3. secretary bird

Synonyms[edit]

  • (bird): secretarisvogel
