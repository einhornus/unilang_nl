stokbrood (37713)


Etymology

Compound of stok (“stick”) +‎ brood (“bread”). First attested in the 1910s in advertisements, in particular in the Dutch East Indies and Delft">Delft, but seemingly entering the common vocabulary only in the 1960s and 1970s.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈstɔk.broːt/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: stok‧brood

Noun

stokbrood n (plural stokbroden, diminutive stokbroodje n)

  1. baguette (elongated French type of bread)
    • 1962, Pieter Kornelis Huibregtse, Groen land, zwarte mensen, page 70.
      Even verder draagt er een drie met een touwtje samengebonden stokbroden.
      A little further away someone carries three baguettes that have been tied together with a piece of string.

Zelfstandig naamwoord

stokbrood o

  1. (voeding) lang smal brood met knapperige korst
Synoniemen
