vatten (7833)


Etymology

From earlier vaten, from Middle Dutch vaten, from Old Dutch">Old Dutch *faton, from Proto-Germanic *fatōną. Equivalent to vat +‎ -en. The short a was introduced by analogy with the uninflected singular form of the noun vat.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈvɑtə(n)/
  • audio
    		(file)
  • Rhymes: -ɑtən

Verb

vatten

  1. (transitive) to grasp
  2. (transitive) to grasp mentally, to understand
  3. (transitive) to catch, to apprehend
  4. (transitive) to catch, to be afflicted with (a disease)
