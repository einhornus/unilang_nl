  • Hyphenation: af‧val

Etymology 1[edit]

From Middle Dutch afval, shortening of slachtafval (“that which fell off from slaughtered meat.”), equivalent to af +‎ val. Compare English offal.

Noun[edit]

afval m or n (uncountable)

  1. rubbish, garbage, trash
    Morgen zullen zij het afval ophalen.
    Tomorrow, they will collect the garbage.

Noun[edit]

afval m (plural afvallen)

  1. The act of dropping out (of a race etc.)
  2. apostasy, the act of rejecting a previous affiliation or belief
