hermitage (75431)



Etymology

From Old French ermitage, from Medieval Latin hermitagium. See English hermit.

Noun

hermitage (plural hermitages)

  1. A house or dwelling where a hermit lives.
  2. A place of seclusion.
  3. A period of seclusion.
