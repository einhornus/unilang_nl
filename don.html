Etymology

From Proto-Germanic *dōną (“to do”), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰeh₁- (“to make, do, place”). Cognate with Old Frisian dwā, Old Saxon dōn, Old Dutch duon, High German">Old High German goh">tuon. Non-Germanic cognates include Ancient Greek">Ancient Greek τίθημι (títhēmi), Latin faciō, Church Slavonic">Old Church Slavonic дѣти (děti).
