lefgozer (31759)


Etymology

From lef (“courage”) +‎ gozer (“guy”).

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈlɛfˌxoː.zər/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: lef‧go‧zer

Noun

lefgozer m (plural lefgozers, diminutive lefgozertje n)

  1. (informal, chiefly Netherlands) Someone with a lot of chutzpah; a daring or impudent person.
    Synonyms: durfal, waaghals
