droef (45591)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch droeve, droue, from Old Dutch *drōvi, *druovi, from Proto-Germanic *drōbuz (“turbid”). Compare English droff and German trüb.

Pronunciation

  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Rhymes: -uf

Adjective

droef (comparative droever, superlative droefst)

  1. sad, miserable
    Jij maakt mij droef.
    You make me sad.
