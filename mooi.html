mooi (194)


Etymology

From Middle Dutch moy, from Old Dutch">Old Dutch *mōi (“nice, pleasant, clean”), from Proto-Germanic *mawjaz (“clean, washed”), from Proto-Indo-European *mewh₂- (“to wash, wet”). Cognate with Low German">Middle Low German moie (“nice, sheen”) (Low German">Low German moi (“pretty, good, nice”)), Low German">Middle Low German mūten (“to wash the face”), and possibly to Latin mundus (“clean, neat, elegant”). Adjective

mooi (comparative mooier, superlative mooist)

  1. beautiful, pretty, handsome
    Wat een mooi meisje. ― What a beautiful girl.
    Ik ben te mooi om te werken. ― I'm too beautiful to work.
  2. nice, good
    Mooi werk! ― Nice work!
