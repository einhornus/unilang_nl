ox (18182)


Etymology

From Middle English oxe, from Old English ang">oxa, from Proto-Germanic *uhsô (compare West Frisian okse, Dutch os, German Ochse), from Proto-Indo-European *uksḗn. Cognate with Welsh language">Welsh ych (“ox”), Tocharian A ops, Tocharian B okso (“draft-ox”), Avestan 𐬎𐬑𐬱𐬀𐬥‎ (uxšan, “bull”), Sanskrit उक्षन् (ukṣán).

Pronunciation

  • (General American">General American) IPA(key): /ˈɑks/
  • (Received Pronunciation) IPA(key): /ˈɒks/
  • Rhymes: -ɒks
  • Audio (Canada)
    		(file)

Noun

ox (plural oxen)

Two oxen.
  1. An adult castrated male of cattle (B. taurus).
  2. Any bovine animal (genus Bos). A neat, a beef.
    • 1934, commentary on the Qur'an (Sura 39 verse 6) by Abdullah Yusuf Ali:
      Here the same four kinds are mentioned ... These are sheep, goats, camels and oxen.

Synonyms

  • (castrated male of cattle): bullock, steer

Related terms

  • aurochs (from *ūrô (“aurochs”) + *uhsô (“ox”)
