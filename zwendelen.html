zwendelen (96429)



Etymology

From zwindelen, from Middle Dutch swindelen. Equivalent to zwinden (“to dissapear”) +‎ -elen. The sense “to swindle” is a semantic loan from German schwindeln and possibly English swindle.[1]

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /ˈzʋɛndələ(n)/
  • Audio
    		(file)
  • Hyphenation: zwen‧de‧len
  • Rhymes: -ɛndələn

Verb

zwendelen

  1. (intransitive) to swindle [from 18th c.]
  2. (intransitive, obsolete) to be dizzy
