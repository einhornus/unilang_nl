snoop (29055)


Etymology

From Dutch snoepen (“to pry, eat in secret, sneak”). Related to Dutch and Low German">Low German snappen (“to bite, seize”), Dutch snavel (“beak, bill, pecker, neb”), German Schnabel (“beak, bill, mouth”). More at snap.

Pronunciation

  • IPA(key): /snuːp/
  • EnPR: sno͞op
  • Rhymes: -uːp

Verb

snoop (third-person singular simple present snoops, present participle snooping, simple past and past participle past|and|past|ptcp-form-of" lang="en">snooped)

  1. To be devious and cunning so as not to be seen.
  2. To secretly spy on or investigate, especially into the private personal life of others.
    If I had not snooped on her, I wouldn't have found out that she lied about her degree.

Related terms

  • snoop around

Noun

snoop (plural snoops)

  1. The act of snooping
  2. One who snoops
    Be careful what you say around Gene because he's the bosses' snoop.
  3. A private detective
    She hired a snoop to find out if her husband was having an affair.
